My Approach

Teaching Philosophy

"Psychology should be experienced, not memorised."

— Jyotika Rai

I believe the most powerful learning happens when students encounter psychology in the context of their own lives. Every concept I teach is anchored in real-world application, encouraging students to observe, reflect, and question.

My classroom is built around five core principles that transform psychology from a subject to be passed into a lens through which students understand the world:

  • Case-Based Learning — Real cases make abstract theories concrete and memorable
  • Practical Activities — Students conduct experiments, interviews, and observations
  • Critical Thinking — Every claim is examined, not accepted at face value
  • Real-Life Applications — Linking theory to career, relationships, and mental health
  • Visual Learning — Diagrams, infographics, and Canva-designed visuals for every topic

Academic Work

Research

Dissertation  ·  Master's Research

Relationship Between Grit, Resilience, and Mental Well-being Among Young Adults

Objective

To examine how grit and resilience independently and jointly predict mental well-being outcomes in young adult populations.

Methodology

Quantitative correlational design using validated psychometric tools and inferential statistical analysis.

Sample

Young adults across educational settings, assessed using standardised measures for all three constructs.

Key Finding

Grit and resilience together were significant predictors of mental well-being, underscoring positive psychology's role in student support.
